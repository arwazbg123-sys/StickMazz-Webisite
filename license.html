▶ What is MIT License?

The MIT License is one of the most popular permissive open-source licenses in the world. It was created by the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and has become a standard for many projects globally.

Key Characteristics:

  • Permissive: Allows both private and commercial use
  • Minimal Restrictions: Few limitations on how you can use the software
  • Condition-Based: Requires attribution and includes a liability clause
  • Legally Binding: Protects both creator and user with clear terms
  • Globally Recognized: Accepted by developers, companies, and legal professionals worldwide

▶ ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use StickMazz Wiki content commercially?
Yes! The MIT License allows commercial use. You can create commercial products, services, or businesses using StickMazz Wiki content. Just remember to provide attribution to Naufal MrSov and include the MIT License notice.
Do I need to share my modifications?
No. Unlike some open-source licenses, MIT doesn't require you to share your modifications. You can modify the content and keep your changes private. However, if you distribute the modified version, you must include the license.
What if someone creates a derivative work?
They must include the original copyright notice and MIT License. They should clearly indicate what changes they made. They can use it commercially and don't need to share their modifications, but must include attribution.
Can I change the license terms?
No. You cannot change the license of the original content. If you distribute unmodified content, it remains MIT Licensed. You can only change the license of your own original additions, but derivative works must still include the original MIT License.
Is MIT License suitable for commercial projects?
Absolutely! MIT License is widely used in commercial projects including large companies. It provides clear legal terms while maintaining permissive use. Many enterprises prefer MIT for its simplicity and legal clarity.
What about patents and trademarks?
MIT License doesn't grant any patent rights or trademark permissions. If you use StickMazz characters or branding, you may need separate permission beyond the MIT License. The license covers the content/code itself.
Can I use this for creating games?
Yes! You can create games using StickMazz Wiki lore, characters, and assets under the MIT License. Just provide clear attribution to Naufal MrSov and include the license notice in your game's credits or documentation.
